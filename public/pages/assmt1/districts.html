Districts of Kerala

Alappuzha

Alappuzha is best known for houseboat cruises along the rustic Kerala backwaters, a network of tranquil canals and lagoons.

Ernakulam

Ernakulam is known for Marine Drive, a busy waterfront promenade where boats offer backwater cruises..

Idukki

Idukki district is a densely forested, mountainous region in the south Indian state of Kerala.

Kannur

Kannur is the cradle of Theyyam is a lively melange of beautiful beaches, historic sites, interesting folk arts and music.

Kasaragod

Kasargod stands unique among other districts in Kerala. Kasargod is the land of seven languages and mixture of several cultures.

Kollam

Kollam otherwise called as the Cashew Town is situated in the southwest corner of India in Kerala. Kollam has an eventful past. Kollam was an international emporium of trade.

Kottayam

Kottayam is a wonderful district in Central Kerala bordered by the lofty Western Ghats on the east and the Vembanad Lake and paddy fields of Kuttanad on the west.

Kozhikode

Kozhikode, The Harbour of Honesty was once the capital of powerful Zamorins and an important trading centre in the Malabar region.

Malappuram

Malappuram district in Kerala located between Kozhikode and Palakkad districts is a popular tourist attraction with its stunning natural beauty and glorious past.

Palakkad

Palakkad also called as Palghat is the gateway to Kerala at the foot of the western ghats. Palakkad is a land of green paddy fields and graceful tall coconut trees.

Pathanamthitta

Pathanamthitta, the smallest district in God’s own country is renowned as the centre of Pilgrim Worship in Kerala.

Thiruvananthapuram

Thiruvananthapuram, the charming capital city of God’s Own Country is the best place to start Kerala tour. It is a must visit place in Kerala.

Thrissur

Thrissur, the quiet small town is the cultural capital of Kerala. Ideally located at the central region of the Kerala, Thrissur is popular for its annual festival called Thrissur Pooram.

Wayanad

Wayanad is often called as the green paradise in God’s own country “Kerala”. It is a hill station, 700 meters to 2000 meters above sea level, in northern part of Kerala with the mist clad peaks.
